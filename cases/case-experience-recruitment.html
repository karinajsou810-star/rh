The context

In the employment and vocational training sector, my job was the match: reading what employers actually needed, reading who candidates actually were, and connecting the right person to the right role.

The real problem

Filling a role is easy. Matching a person to it, the one who'll actually fit and stay, is the hard part.

A referral that looks good on paper but doesn't fit costs everyone, the employer, the candidate, and the reputation of whoever made the match. So I optimised for fit, not just availability.

What the work looked like

A high-volume pipeline, run with a quality-first eye.
