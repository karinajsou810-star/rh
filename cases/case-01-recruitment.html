Case 01 · Talent Acquisition

Inside a high‑performing recruitment engine

How a specialised search firm makes placements that actually stick, mapped end to end.

Focus
Talent attraction & retention
Sector
Executive search
My role
Field research & analysis
Method
Executive interview + process mapping

The context

I studied a specialised executive‑search firm whose reputation rested on one thing most recruiters can't promise: placements that last. In a market obsessed with speed and volume, their candidates stayed. I set out to understand why.

The real question

Most recruiters optimise for the placement. The best ones optimise for what happens after it.

Anyone can fill a seat. The hard part, and the expensive one, is filling it with someone who stays. I wanted to map what a hiring process looks like when retention is the goal, not the by‑product.

The approach

A structured field study, built around the people closest to the process.
01
Interviewed the executive director
A structured interview to understand their philosophy, methodology, and how they define a successful placement.
02
Mapped the end‑to‑end process
From role diagnosis and sourcing to candidate preparation, placement, and post‑hire follow‑up.
03
Traced the candidate journey
How a candidate is assessed, prepared, placed, and supported once inside the client company.
04
Distilled the retention mechanisms
Isolated the specific practices that made their placements stick where others fall apart.

What the study mapped

3
phase process, diagnosis → design → integration
10
recruitment areas covered, role‑specific profiles
3‑mo
post‑hire check‑in cycle with candidate & client
100%
of candidates prepared before placement

Their edge wasn't a clever sourcing trick. It was structure: a profile designed for each role rather than a generic ad, candidates coached before they ever met the client, and a relationship that continued for months after the hire, checking in, catching problems early, and feeding what they learned back into the next placement.

The takeaway

Hiring doesn't end at the offer. The firms that keep people treat placement as the middle of the process, never the end.

It's the same principle I bring to growing teams: a great hire is the start of retention, not the finish line of recruiting.
