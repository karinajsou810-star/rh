The challenge

The interviews happened. The information disappeared.

The institute ran recruitment interviews remotely — which allowed geographic flexibility and scale. But what happened inside each interview stayed in the memory of whoever conducted it. No structured record. No way to compare candidates against consistent criteria. When the team needed to make a decision, they depended on scattered notes, subjective impressions, and whoever remembered what.

The problem multiplied across the funnel. After each interview, candidates waited for feedback without knowing how long. Follow-ups depended on someone remembering to do them. And in processes with 40 open roles simultaneously, the probability of a qualified candidate falling between phases due to lack of communication was high — and costly.

With a market average time-to-hire of 23 to 45 days, the team knew their process could be faster. But without data on where time was being lost, improvement was impossible to measure — or sustain.

"There were processes that depended on whoever had conducted the interview being available to explain what they had seen. The decision about a candidate was stuck in a person, not in a system."

Ana Barreiro · HR, Public Employment and Training Institute

The solution

AI started recording every interview. The team started deciding with data.

The institute now has an interview intelligence system that transforms each remote session into a structured, actionable record — automatically, with no manual work from the team.

Each interview generates information that enters the recruitment pipeline the moment the session ends. The team doesn't need to write notes, update any system, or remember what was said. What was volatile and memory-dependent became consistent, shared, and available to the entire team.

Candidate communication became automatic and immediate. When a candidate advances, they receive confirmation and next steps without anyone writing anything. When they don't advance, they receive feedback with respect and clarity — also with no manual intervention. No candidate is left without a response.

"From the moment the system went live, the team started arriving at each decision with structured information instead of impressions. And candidates started receiving an immediate response — not when someone remembered."

Ana Barreiro · HR, Public Employment and Training Institute
