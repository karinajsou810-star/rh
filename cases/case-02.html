The challenge

Running the office had quietly become a full-time job.

An executive office runs on details: schedules, travel, suppliers, events, and the small team that keeps everything moving. Scheduling, coverage, supplier coordination, and welcoming new team members all ran on top of an already full day to day, and each one was done a little differently depending on the day.

When someone was away, coverage was improvised at the last minute. When a new team member arrived, they learned by following whoever had time to explain. And because nothing was documented, every answer lived in someone's head, which meant every absence, every departure, and every busy week put the whole routine at risk.

None of it was anyone's fault. It is simply what happens when an office grows faster than its processes.

"We were good at our jobs, but everything depended on memory and goodwill. If two things went wrong on the same day, the whole routine felt it. What we needed was not more effort. It was structure."
Benedita Jerônimo · Operations professional
The solution

People operations, documented and implemented.

The office now runs on one clear rhythm. Schedules and coverage are planned ahead, so absences are covered before they happen instead of patched on the day. Suppliers, events, and travel each have an owner and a deadline, not a mental note.

Every new team member arrives to a structured welcome: a plan for their first days, the routines of the office documented step by step, and someone responsible for them. Nobody learns by guessing anymore, and new team members are ready from day one.

And the team is looked after, not just organized. Regular check-ins, clear expectations, and honest conversations about workload turned a group of individuals into a team that stays.

"Karina brought structure and calm to everything: the schedules, the suppliers, the way new colleagues were welcomed. She looked after the people the same way she looked after the details. With her, you always knew where things stood."
Ticiana Schultz · Personal assistant, executive office
