The challenge

A recruitment process that only worked when the right person was in the room.

The consultancy had a solid market proposition: specialist recruitment of qualified technicians and middle management, with close candidate follow-up from application through the first months in the new role.

The problem was that this process existed in people's heads, not in a shared system. Each consultant managed their pipeline differently. There were no common screening criteria, no stages with defined owners, no delivery cycle with a reference deadline. What worked well when one person did everything started to strain as the team grew and processes multiplied.

With national and international expansion underway, the risk was clear: more volume, more consultants, less consistency.

"If we want someone to wear our organisation's shirt, we should be the ones to teach them how to wear it."

Executive Director · Specialist recruitment consultancy

The solution

A recruitment process the entire team can execute to the same standard.

I was contracted to create what the company didn't have: a clear, shared recruitment structure that's independent of who executes it. I started by understanding how recruitment actually worked in practice — not how it was supposed to work on paper.

The result was a process with defined stages, clear criteria by profile type, and responsibilities assigned at each point. Each consultant now knows what to do, when to do it, and what to deliver to the client at each phase. The post-placement follow-up cycle — practised informally — was formalised and integrated as part of the standard process.

Opening a new market or onboarding a new consultant no longer means reinventing how the work is done. The standard exists. It's documented. And it can be replicated.

"Our recruitment is proximity-based and lifelong. Our work doesn't begin at attraction and doesn't end with placement."

Executive Director · Specialist recruitment consultancy
