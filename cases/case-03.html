The challenge

Interviews that should never have happened were costing time, money, and the best candidates.

In a public employment institute with more than one thousand active candidates simultaneously, the recruitment process ran entirely manually. Every application was triaged one by one. Responses took days. And without a pre-selection system, candidates without the minimum requirements reached the interview stage — consuming time from recruiters, managers, and qualified candidates who waited longer than they should.

The cost wasn't just operational. The more time the team spent in unqualified interviews, the less time they had to identify and prepare the right profiles. Allocation rates suffered. Deadlines slipped. And the most qualified candidates — those with more options — dropped out while waiting for a response.

"We had three processes that didn't talk to each other: recruitment, onboarding, and scheduling. Candidates advanced through the process depending on someone pushing them manually."

Ana Barreiro · HR, Public Employment and Training Institute

The solution

AI handles triage. The team handles recruitment.

The institute now has an intelligent pre-selection system that manages the top of the funnel autonomously. Each application is evaluated the moment it arrives. Profiles that don't meet requirements receive an immediate response. Those that advance enter the pipeline already qualified — ready for interview, with no manual triage work in between.

AI eliminates the work that consumes the most time and creates the least value: reading repetitive applications, filtering out-of-criteria profiles, writing rejection responses. The recruitment team enters the process where their judgement is irreplaceable — evaluating borderline profiles, preparing candidates, and managing client relationships.

The team doesn't do less work. They do different work. Candidates advance without depending on someone to push them manually.
